The Impact of Technology on Youth Mental Health

Question 1 of 15
Comprehension
What is the primary concern about social media use among teenagers?
Explanation

Research shows that excessive social media use correlates with increased anxiety, depression, and body image issues among teenagers due to constant comparison and FOMO.

Question 2 of 15
Vocabulary
What does "FOMO" stand for?
Explanation

FOMO is the anxiety teenagers feel when they see others having experiences they're not part of. This drives compulsive social media checking and contributes to mental health issues.

Question 3 of 15
Grammar: Passive Voice
Complete: "Teenagers ___"
Explanation

The passive voice "are constantly exposed" emphasizes that teenagers are receiving/experiencing something done to them, which is the focus in discussing how social media affects them.

Question 4 of 15
Grammar: Reporting Verbs
Complete: "Studies ___"
Explanation

"Have shown" is the correct reporting verb form. It indicates that studies have demonstrated/revealed this fact. This structure is essential for academic and medical writing.

Question 5 of 15
Comprehension
How does blue light from screens affect sleep?
Explanation

Blue light tricks the brain into thinking it's daytime, suppressing melatonin (the sleep hormone). This is why screen use before bed disrupts sleep cycles, especially in teenagers.

Question 6 of 15
Vocabulary
What is "cyberbullying"?
Explanation

Cyberbullying involves repeated harassment online. It's particularly damaging because it's persistent, public, and can reach victims anywhere, anytime.

Question 7 of 15
Grammar: Passive Voice
Complete: "Mental health issues ___"
Explanation

"Are often ignored" is the correct passive voice. It emphasizes that mental health issues are being overlooked, which is the focus of the discussion.

Question 8 of 15
Critical Thinking
Is technology inherently bad for mental health?
Explanation

Technology is a tool. The problem isn't technology itself but how it's used—excessive consumption, lack of boundaries, and poor digital literacy. Healthy use can be beneficial.

Question 9 of 15
Vocabulary
What is a "digital detox"?
Explanation

A digital detox is intentional time away from screens and devices. Research shows even short breaks improve sleep, focus, and mental well-being.

Question 10 of 15
Grammar: Reporting Verbs
Complete: "Psychologists ___"
Explanation

"Argue" is a reporting verb used to present claims or positions. "Psychologists argue that..." is the standard academic structure for presenting expert opinions.

Question 11 of 15
Comprehension
What role do algorithms play in social media's impact on mental health?
Explanation

Algorithms prioritize engagement over well-being. They often amplify divisive, anxiety-inducing, or comparison-heavy content because it keeps users scrolling longer.

Question 12 of 15
Vocabulary
What does "mental health literacy" mean?
Explanation

Mental health literacy includes recognizing signs of mental health issues, understanding treatment options, and knowing how to seek professional help. It's crucial for teenagers navigating digital stress.

Question 13 of 15
Grammar: Passive Voice
Complete: "Screen time limits ___"
Explanation

"Are recommended" is the correct passive voice. It emphasizes that screen time limits are being advised/suggested by experts, which is the focus of the statement.

Question 14 of 15
Critical Thinking
What is the most effective approach to addressing technology's impact on youth mental health?
Explanation

A holistic approach is most effective: education, healthy boundaries, family involvement, and professional mental health support. No single solution works for everyone.
